Strategies

Three battle-tested strategies. Or write your own.

Each ships with documented logic, tunable parameters, and live signal markers. Extend the BaseStrategy interface and register a new one in a single line.

Exploits mean reversion — prices that stretch too far from their average tend to snap back. RSI measures the speed and magnitude of moves on a 0–100 scale.

▲ BUY

RSI(14) drops below oversold (30) — price is exhausted, likely to bounce.

▼ SELL

RSI(14) rises above overbought (70) — the rally is extended, likely to fade.

◈ FILTER

Optional Bollinger confirmation — price must touch the lower / upper band.

Best for
Sideways, range-bound markets. Index ETFs like SPY, QQQ.
Avoid when
Strong trends — RSI can stay oversold for a long time.

A trend-following crossover system. When the fast EMA pushes above the slow EMA, momentum is shifting up — a golden cross. The reverse is a death cross.

▲ BUY

EMA(9) crosses above EMA(21) and volume > average × multiplier.

▼ SELL

EMA(9) crosses below EMA(21) — momentum has rolled over.

◈ VOLUME

The move must be confirmed by above-average volume to avoid whipsaws.

Best for
Trending markets with momentum. Tech names, crypto.
Avoid when
Choppy, sideways tape — false crossovers pile up.

An intraday momentum play. VWAP is the institutional “fair value” benchmark. A break above it on unusual volume signals real buying pressure.

▲ BUY

Price breaks above VWAP and volume z-score > threshold (1.5).

▼ SELL

Price falls back below VWAP — the breakout failed or the target is hit.

◈ Z-SCORE

z = (volume − avg) / std — filters out low-conviction noise.

Best for
Liquid large-caps, intraday, high-volume market open.
Avoid when
Daily charts (VWAP resets) and thin pre/post-market.

    signal = BUY

    signal = SELL
